# CS Hardware Day 4 - Wave

In this lesson, participants explore how repeating electrical signals create waves, sound, timing, and communication systems. Building on previous lessons about voltage, transistors, and capacitors, participants investigate oscillators, integrated circuits, and signal visualization using the 555 Timer IC and an oscilloscope. Through hands-on sound and timing circuits, participants connect electrical oscillation to speakers, frequency, digital signaling, and modern computer communication systems. The lesson emphasizes experimentation, visualization, debugging, and the relationship between electrical waves and information.

[Slides](https://dpi-tta-slides.github.io/cs-hardware-4-wave/)

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, participants will be able to:

- Explain what an Integrated Circuit (IC) is
- Describe how the 555 Timer IC generates oscillations
- Build and troubleshoot a 555 Timer oscillator circuit
- Explain how resistance and capacitance affect oscillation frequency
- Use an oscilloscope to visualize electrical signals
- Identify voltage, time, frequency, and wave shape on an oscilloscope
- Explain how speakers convert electrical signals into sound
- Build a simple electronic sound/noise maker circuit
- Relate oscillators to clocks, sound systems, and communication systems
- Explain how digital systems use changing voltage levels to represent information
- Distinguish between power, ground, and signal/data wires in electronics
